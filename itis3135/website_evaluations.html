Website Evaluations

Jacob's Wonderful Web Development Work

HP.com

HP’s website offers a wide variety of computing and printing-related solutions. Customers can efficiently order laptops, desktops, printers, or even support-related services from this website. HP also offers its products at various retail stores. HP is known worldwide for its wide range of products initially stated above.

  • Contrast: The readability of HP’s website is very easy. Elements do appear to be similar which helps me read the contents of the webpage to find the specific product I want quickly and easily. The elements do seem to follow the principles really well! Images are less bright with darker text to improve readability.
  • Repetition: The font and visual elements, in general, appear to follow this principle extremely well. The website is easy to navigate, and clicking the shop button creates a red banner at the top of the screen to increase the chances of the users clicking on the “SHOP” button which includes text referring to a sale and free shipping.
  • Alignment: The alignment of HP’s website is very easy for users to navigate. Everything appears to be grouped by columns. This helps users find exactly what they want without scanning the page needlessly for the information they are looking for.
  • Proximity: The proximity of elements on HP’s website appears to be well-spaced but also fits in a professional manner. Everything is easy for a user to navigate, and the alignment of the elements is equal.

TheBigUglyWebsite.com

TheBigUglyWebsite was created as an example to showcase the horrors of not following C.R.A.P. principles. This website appears to do its job pretty well, and clicking the hyperlinks in the website brings the user other webpages to showcase how UGLY they are!

  • Contrast: The contrast of this website isn’t that great. The color of the background and text on the website startup appear normal, but taking a look at the images can completely throw users off.
  • Repetition: Images do appear to be repeated, but they aren’t following CRAPS principles. The colors of the images and text vary, similarly, the size of the images appear to be all over the place.
  • Alignment: The alignment of the images and text on this webpage appears to be everywhere. Images overlap other images. The alignment of everything on the webpage appears to be towards the left of the webpage. This means the whole right side of this webpage appears to be blank.
  • Proximity: The proximity of the elements on this webpage appears to be pretty close together. The elements feel pretty unprofessional since images are thrown in between random subject headings, and some images even overlap with mismatching colors. Some of the subject headings appear to also have mismatching colors and animations (increased / decreased size, etc.).

C.R.A.P. Principles - Notes

Contrast:

  1. Elements that aren’t the same should be very different so they stand out and are easy to read.
  2. Contrast helps visibility. Making them only slightly different can make your work hard to read.
  3. Contrast draws attention and improves readability.

Repetition:

  1. Repeating visual elements of design throughout the piece.
  2. This brings a sense of uniformity and professionalism to the work.
  3. This includes: colors, fonts, font size, shapes, and line thickness.

Alignment:

  1. All elements should line up and be organized.
  2. Every element should have some visual connection with another element on the page.
  3. Straight alignment or tilt alignment.
  4. Alignment ORGANIZES your work

Proximity:

  1. Elements that are related should be grouped together.
  2. Separate design elements should have enough space in between to communicate they are different.
  3. Grouping elements together helps your reader know where to look.
